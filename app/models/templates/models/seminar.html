Kipoi - Seminar

The monthly virtual seminar series is designed as a platform for interested Kipoi users and developers and will host talks on the applications of deep learning on biological data. The seminar is held on every first Wednesday of the month at 5:30 p.m. - 6:30 p.m. CET. We are also happy to share the recordings of the seminar on YouTube.

How to take part

The Virtual Seminar Series takes place via Zoom. To take part in the seminar, you can register for the online Zoom conference. Your personal join link will be valid for all upcoming lectures of the series.

Next seminar

Title: Resolving the genomic, spatial, phenotypic and cell type heterogeneity of (tumor) tissues.
3 March 2023 5:30 p.m. - 6:30 p.m. Central European Time

Speaker: Ewa Szczurek, University of Warsaw

Abstract:

Biological tissues are characterized by a heterogeneous architecture, displayed on four different levels: genomic, cell type, spatial, and phenotypic. The genomic heterogeneity is particularly important for cancer tissues. As a result of evolution, tumor cells carry genetic changes and can be assigned to clones with different genotypes. Cell type heterogeneity corresponds to the fact that tissues are composed of different cell types. Spatial heterogeneity is exhibited by the organization of different clones or cell types in tissue space. Finally, phenotypic heterogeneity corresponds to the fact that the different clones or cell types have different marker genes and different expression profiles. I will present recent probabilistic graphical model-based methods developed in my lab, devoted to resolving genomic heterogeneity and tumor evolution from single cell DNA sequencing data (SIEVE [1] and CONET [2]), as well as joint inference of spatial, phenotypic and genomic (Tumoroscope [3]) or cell type (Celloscope [4]) heterogeneity of tissues from spatial transcriptomics data.

[2] M. Markowska, T. Cakala, B. Miasojedow, B. Aybey, D. Juraeva, J. Mazur, E. Ross, E. Staub, E. Szczurek, CONET: Copy number event tree model of evolutionary tumor history for single-cell data, Genome Biology, 2022, 23 (1), 1-35
